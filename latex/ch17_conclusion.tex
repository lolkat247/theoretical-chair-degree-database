\section{Conclusion}

This database gives TCD Stocking Inc. a single and orderly record of its daily trade. Products, vendors, warehouses, customers, orders, shipments, order lines, inventory, and order status history are each kept in their proper place, so the same fact does not have to be copied from table to table. This makes the system easier to trust. A manager can see what is in stock, where it is stored, who supplied it, who bought it, and how an order moved through the business. The result is not a grand machine, but a useful one: it records the facts clearly and lets the business act on them.

The design also supports the company's need for accounting and control. Invoice totals are derived from order line quantities, unit prices, warehouse shipping costs, and tax calculations, rather than stored as loose figures that could become false over time. The status history table preserves how each order changed, which gives the company a time-based record for customer service, taxes, and review. By using keys, constraints, subtypes, views, and tested queries, the database reduces confusion and gives TCD Stocking Inc. a practical foundation for running its wholesale operation with better records and fewer hidden errors.
